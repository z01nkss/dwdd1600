Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the practice of using technology safely, responsibly, ethically, and effectively in online spaces - at school, at work, and in everyday life.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is the set of rules and laws that govern how people use technology, the internet, and digital content.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy is a set of rules that explain how technology, devices, and networks may and may not be used within an organization like a school, workplace, or website.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's work, ideas,or words as your own wihtout giving proper credit.

It doesn't matter whether it's done on purpose or by accident - it's still plagiarism.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair advantage or misrepresents their learning

In simple terms: it's cheating or being dishonest in schoolwork.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content may be used only in responsible ways. AI is a tool to support learning - not a replacement for thinking or original work.
